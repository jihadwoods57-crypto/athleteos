# OnStandard — Landing Page Rewrite (Coach-First)

*Generated 2026-07-15 from `.agents/product-marketing.md` + CRO audit. Voice: direct, earned, coach, plain-spoken, no hype, no invented numbers.*

## The one decision driving this rewrite

The page's money conversion is **a coach or facility signing up**. They pay; they bring the free roster. The current hero (*"Prove the work / visible to the people counting on you"*) is written to the **athlete** — who can't convert here. So the highest-value visitor lands on copy meant for someone else.

Everything below re-aims the page at the coach, keeps "Prove the work" as the brand line, and pulls the sharpest assets (the "not a tracker" triad, the founder story, the Founding 50 scarcity) up to where the decision actually gets made.

### Variant B — "Proof / credibility"
**Headline:** Talent is common. Proof isn't.
**Subhead:** Turn every athlete's daily work into a score you can actually trust — one fixed formula, photo-verified, impossible to game. See who's executing without chasing anyone.
*Rationale: leans on differentiation; strong for the skeptical veteran coach who's been burned by soft tools.*

### Variant C — "Keep brand line, add the job"
**Headline:** Prove the work.
**Subhead:** The accountability platform that shows coaches who's executing — verified from a photo, scored on one standard, live. Not a tracker. Not a feed. Proof.
*Rationale: preserves existing brand equity while fixing the who-is-this-for gap in the same breath.*

## HOW IT WORKS (4 steps)

**Header:** Simple to use. Built for execution.
**Intro:** From meals to recovery to check-ins, OnStandard captures the daily actions that drive results. AI reads, verifies, and surfaces what matters — and the score moves the moment the work lands.

1. **Log the day.** Point the camera at the plate. Meals, recovery, and check-ins land in seconds. Live capture only — gallery photos never score.
2. **The AI reads & verifies.** Foods, portions, and macros, with a confidence level on every detection. When it isn't sure, it shows a "?" — never a guess.
3. **The score moves.** The day lands against the standard and the score reflects what actually got done. On time counts full. Late counts half. Skipped reads as skipped.
4. **The right people see it.** Coaches and trainers get the whole roster in three seconds: who's on standard, who's slipping, every log as it lands.

*Rationale: keep as-is — it's clear and the "live capture only / never a guess / honest late credit" details are your anti-gaming proof. Consider adding a one-line header above this: "Why it can't be faked" to make the anti-gaming story explicit (see CRO note #7).*

## AI HONESTY

**Header:** The AI reads. It never decides.

- **It reads honestly.** Per-food confidence on every plate; a "?" instead of a guess. It can flag possible allergens from what it detects and what you've declared — but it can never confirm a plate is allergen-free. Don't rely on it for that.
- **It talks in the thread.** Every log opens a discussion: the AI's read, then your coach, then you. It answers from your plan and your day — not the internet.
- **It never decides.** It doesn't set targets, doesn't touch the score, and never invents a number a person acts on. Fixed math scores the day. Your professional sets the bar.

*Rationale: unchanged — this is credibility and liability-cover in one. In a market wary of "AI slop," "the AI never decides" is a differentiator, not a limitation.*

# Hero A/B Test Plan

**Reality check first:** a pre-launch site has low traffic, so this is a **directional/qualitative** test, not a stat-sig machine. Don't wait for p<0.05 you'll never reach — decide on direction + qualitative signal, and treat the hero as the highest-leverage thing to get roughly right, then move on.

**Hypothesis:** A coach-first hero (leads with the coach's problem + a value/scarcity CTA) will convert more *coach* founding-access requests than the current athlete-first hero, because the paying visitor sees themselves and the offer in the first five seconds.

**Control (A):** Current hero — "Prove the work. / visible to the people counting on you." + "Request founding access."
**Variant (B):** Recommended hero — "You can't coach what you can't see." + "Claim a Founding 50 spot" + scarcity line.

**Primary metric:** Founding-access requests from coaches/facilities (the money conversion), as a rate of unique visitors.
**Guardrail metric:** Total requests including athletes — make sure re-aiming at coaches doesn't tank overall signups or confuse athletes arriving via coach referral. Also watch scroll-depth/bounce on the hero.

**Segment if you can:** tag requests by role (coach / trainer / program / athlete / parent) at form submit. The whole point is *coach* conversion — a blended number can hide the win.

**How to run it on low traffic:**
- 50/50 split, run until you have enough *coach* requests to see a clear directional gap (e.g. one variant roughly doubling the other over a meaningful window), OR ~2–4 weeks, whichever first.
- Pair it with **5–8 qualitative reads**: show both heroes to real coaches (DMs, calls, founder network) and ask "which one is for you, and what does this company do?" Qualitative clarity often decides a pre-launch hero faster than traffic will.

**How to call it:**
- Ship B if it wins on coach-request rate **and** holds the guardrail (no collapse in total/athlete signups).
- If flat on numbers but coaches clearly understand B faster in the qualitative reads → ship B anyway (clarity compounds later).
- If B underperforms → keep A's brand line but test Variant C (keeps "Prove the work," adds the who-it's-for clause) before concluding athlete-first was right.
