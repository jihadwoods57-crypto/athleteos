## 1. App Name / Title

**iOS (30 char): `OnStandard: Athlete Proof`  → 25 char**

**Google Play (30 char): `OnStandard: Athlete Proof`  → 25 char**

Rationale:
- **Brand first** (`OnStandard`, 10 char) — required so directed installs (an athlete told "get OnStandard" by their coach) find the right app instantly. Most of our installs are *directed*, not discovered, so the brand token has to be exact and clean.
- **`Athlete`** is the single highest-relevance searchable noun for us and it combines with keyword-field terms. Apple indexes each word and auto-combines across title + subtitle + keyword field, so `Athlete` (title) + `accountability` (keyword field) lets us rank for the long-tail **"athlete accountability"** without spending a title slot on the 14-char word "accountability."
- **`Proof`** is the differentiator word, low-competition, and ownable. It carries the spine ("prove the work") while staying a real index token. We deliberately did **not** burn the title on the pure slogan "Prove the Work" — a Challenger app can't afford a keyword-empty title.
- **No repeats** between title and subtitle (Apple indexes a word once). Title owns `athlete` + `proof`; subtitle owns `coach` + `team` + `nutrition` + `verified`. Zero waste.
- Google-legal: no emoji, no ALL CAPS, no "best/#1/free," no CTA. Passes.

Considered and rejected:
- `OnStandard: Team Nutrition` — "nutrition" is higher-volume, but it reads as a *nutrition tracker*, which is exactly what our positioning says we are NOT. We keep "nutrition" in the subtitle/keywords for discovery but refuse to let the title frame us as a food logger.
- `OnStandard: Accountability` — "accountability" is the positioning word but generic across sobriety/habit/finance apps; pairing our own `athlete` with a keyword-field `accountability` gets us the specific phrase without the generic collision.

## 2. Subtitle (iOS) & Short Description (Google Play)

**iOS Subtitle (30 char): `Coach-verified team nutrition`  → 29 char**

Why: three high-intent, non-repeated search tokens in one honest phrase — `coach` (the buyer), `team` (roster/program search), `nutrition` (the 50%-weighted score component and the biggest search category we honestly touch). `verified` is the wedge word — the one thing no competitor can say. Reads as plain truth, not hype.

**Google Play Short Description (80 char): → 76 char**

```
Coach-verified daily execution. Photo-proof nutrition, recovery & check-ins.
```

Why: Google indexes the short description heavily and shows it above the fold, so it front-loads the wedge (`Coach-verified`) then names the three logged surfaces (`nutrition, recovery & check-ins`) so a coach instantly knows what gets proven. `execution`, `photo-proof`, and each surface are all live search tokens. No CTA verb (Google bans them here).

## 3. iOS Keyword Field (100 char)

```
accountability,macros,tracker,meal,log,photo,food,recovery,trainer,dietitian,roster,checkin,sports
```
→ 98 / 100 characters. Comma-separated, no spaces, no word repeated from title/subtitle.

### Keyword research reasoning

Apple auto-combines single words across Title + Subtitle + Keyword field into multi-word phrases and indexes each word once. So the job here is to add the *distinct* words that, combined with `onstandard / athlete / proof / coach / verified / team / nutrition` already in the title+subtitle, unlock the phrases our two audiences actually type.

Who searches, and what they type:

**Coaches / trainers / RDs (the buyers — they browse the store to evaluate a solution):**
- `accountability` → combines with title's `athlete` = **"athlete accountability"**, our owned long-tail.
- `trainer`, `dietitian` → the buyer personas by name; combine with `team`/`nutrition` ("team nutrition," "nutrition trainer").
- `roster` → AMS / team-management intent ("roster nutrition," "team roster").
- `checkin` → the "client check-in app" job that Healthie/Practice Better own; we intercept it.
- `sports` → combines with `nutrition` = **"sports nutrition,"** a high-volume evaluative term.

**Athletes / clients (the users — mostly directed, but some self-search):**
- `macros`, `food`, `meal`, `log`, `photo` → the literal words behind "macro tracker," "meal photo log," "food log." These are the free-side habit searches.
- `recovery` → the 25%-weighted score component; combines with `sleep`-adjacent recovery searches.
- `tracker` → **intercept keyword only.** Positioning forbids calling ourselves a tracker in *visible* copy, but people searching "macro tracker" / "nutrition tracker" are exactly our misrouted audience. We catch the search without ever using the word where a human reads our brand.

Deliberately left out (space is finite): `compliance`, `client`, `performance`, `discipline`, `gym`, `RD`, `habit`. `client`/`compliance` are covered semantically by `checkin`+`roster`; `performance`/`discipline` are brand-voice words but weak as search tokens. Rotate these in via A/B once install data shows which head terms actually convert.

## 6. Category Recommendation

**Primary: Health & Fitness. Secondary: Sports.**

Rationale:
- **Health & Fitness (primary)** is where the search volume and intent we honestly serve actually live — nutrition, recovery, coaching, accountability. Coaches, trainers, and RDs evaluating a solution browse and search here; athletes searching "macro/meal/food log" are here. It maximizes discoverability against the keyword set above.
- **Sports (secondary)** captures the athlete/coach/program identity and the team-roster angle, and it's a less-saturated shelf where a specific "athlete accountability" app can surface in browse/top-charts more easily than in the crowded Health & Fitness charts.
- **Rejected: Business.** It matches the B2B2C revenue model on paper, but neither the coach nor the athlete searches Business to solve this job, and it would strand us away from the nutrition/fitness search terms we rank for. Wrong shelf for discovery.

## 7. Keyword Strategy Note

**Own (long-tail, low competition, high relevance — win these first):**
- **"athlete accountability"** — our defining phrase; almost no one else targets it, and it's exactly what we are. Built from title `athlete` + keyword `accountability`.
- **"the other 167"** — a brand phrase from our own voice. No competition because we coined the frame. Seed it in the description and PR so branded search grows into it.
- **"coach verified nutrition," "photo verified meal log," "athlete check in app," "team nutrition app"** — specific multi-word intents where the self-report incumbents (Healthie, Practice Better, RXFood, TeamBuildr) are weak on the *verification* angle. Our wedge word `verified` is where we can't be matched.

**Intercept (competitive, don't expect to rank #1 — catch misrouted searchers):**
- **"macro tracker," "nutrition tracker," "food log," "meal tracker"** — dominated by MyFitnessPal, MacroFactor, Cronometer. Too competitive to win, but the searcher who's frustrated that trackers only record what they *type in* is our exact convert. We hold the tokens (`tracker`, `macros`, `food`, `meal`, `log`) in the keyword field to appear in the long tail of those searches, and the subtitle/screenshots do the converting ("verified," "proof").

**Too competitive — do not chase in metadata (win via brand/PR/paid instead):**
- **"fitness," "workout," "calorie counter," "diet"** — massive head terms, wrong framing (we're not a fitness/diet app), and unwinnable on ASO alone. Chasing them dilutes relevance and risks miscategorizing us. Leave them out.

**Mechanism:** exploit Apple's cross-field word combination — never repeat a word across title/subtitle/keyword field; let single tokens recombine into phrases. On Google, there's no hidden field, so the same phrases must live naturally in the title, short description, and full description at ~2-3% density (done above) without stuffing.

**Iterate:** post-launch, run one Store Listing Experiment at a time (Google, 7+ days) and Custom Product Pages (iOS) to A/B the subtitle (`team` vs `client` vs `roster`) and screenshot-1 caption. Rotate the benched keywords (`compliance`, `client`, `performance`, `RD`, `habit`) into the iOS field based on which converting terms show up in Search Ads / Console data.
