## 1. Who do we sell to FIRST — gyms or high-school coaches?

**Recommendation: Gyms / performance facilities first. Coaches and schools come next.**

Why: a gym owner can say yes in one conversation (no school committee, no district paperwork),
their clients are usually adults (so we skip the parental-consent legal hurdle), and gym members
become future individual customers down the road. Schools are a great second step once we've
proven it works.

## 2. Is the gym our launch focus, or a "later" thing?

**Recommendation: Make the gym the launch focus — but keep it simple. Ship the basics
(owner dashboard + an opt-in leaderboard members can choose to join). Hold off on the flashy
stuff (lobby-TV displays, points/rewards) until after we've proven people stick around.**

Why: the gym version runs on what we've already built, so it's cheap to launch. The flashy
extras add privacy headaches and can be gamed — not worth it on day one. Win first, sparkle later.

☐ Approve   ☐ Let's talk

## 3. What do we charge? (the starting prices)

**Recommendation: a few tweaks to the numbers — all easy to change since prices are just settings,
not code:**
- **Solo trainer:** add a cheaper entry plan (~$59–79/mo for up to 25 clients) so the small trainer
  with 20 clients says yes. Keep the $124.99 / 50-client plan for busy practices.
- **Individual (a person on their own):** keep $14.99 as the "just graduated, keep your history"
  starter price, but add a higher tier (~$24.99) for people who want their full portable record /
  shareable scholarship card.
- **Gyms & teams:** keep the $249 / $499 / $799 tiers low for now to get people in the door — but
  start tracking the results we deliver so we can justify raising prices later.

Why: $14.99 alone makes us look like just another tracker (the thing we're trying to beat), and the
$125 trainer plan is priced wrong for the solo trainer who's our bread and butter.

☐ Approve the tweaks   ☐ Keep the original numbers   ☐ Let's talk

## 4. What's the ONE thing we want to be known for?

**Recommendation: The morning "here's your game plan for today, here's where you'll finish if you
follow it" — seen by the coach/trainer/parent who cares. Ship it from day one; never cut it.**

Why: it looks forward (not just "here's what you ate"), it's built on our score that nobody can
copy, and the fact that someone is watching *is* the accountability. It's the moment people remember
us by — the way Whoop is known for its "Recovery" number.

☐ Approve   ☐ Let's talk

---

## 5. Stop calling it a "Development Score" in the app (for now)?

**Recommendation: Yes. In the app, name the number for what it honestly measures today
(e.g. "Nutrition Score" / "Execution Score"). Keep "Development Score" as the bigger brand name we
grow into and use in marketing — and earn it later when the number actually measures more.**

Why: today it measures food, water, and a mood slider. Calling that "development" is overselling, and
real users already called it out. A trust brand can't survive "the number lied" in year one.

☐ Approve   ☐ Keep calling it Development Score   ☐ Let's talk

## 6. Start the non-code launch work THIS WEEK?

**Recommendation: Yes — and this is the most important one. Get three things moving:**
1. A lawyer (privacy policy + the rules for handling minors' data).
2. A parent-verification vendor (the thing that confirms a parent is really a parent).
3. An email service (for sign-up confirmations and parent approvals).

Why: the app is built, but it can't legally go live without these — and they take time because other
people do them, not us. Every week they don't start is a week everything else is stuck. This is your
highest-value move right now, more than reviewing any feature.

decides almost everything else (who we build the first version for, what the first sales pitch is,
and which prices to set). Everything else lines up behind it.
