What is OnStandard

Adherence, made visible.

OnStandard is an execution and accountability platform for athletes and coached clients. For a nutrition professional it solves the oldest problem in the field: you can build the perfect plan, but you can't see whether anyone follows it. Here, every plate is photographed, read honestly, timed against its window, and rolled into one daily score — so the conversation moves from "what did you eat?" to "here's what the record shows."

The pipeline

Read → ask → ground → discuss.

1
Detection with confidence

Foods and portions are identified from the live photo, each detection tagged with how sure the model is. Low confidence is shown, not hidden.

2
The clarifying moment

When something invisible would move the macros — cooking fat, a sauce, what's under the plate's edge — the client gets one to three quick questions. "A photo can't show what's hidden under or off the plate."

3
Grounded estimates

Macros are estimates and the system says so — then bounds them with sanity clamps and energy‑consistency checks so one bad read can't spike a day. Clients can correct the breakdown before logging; the photo stays attached either way.

4
A thread, not a verdict

Every log opens a discussion: the AI's read, the professional's comment, the client's reply. The AI answers client questions from their plan and their day — never generic internet nutrition.

How it works

From plate to record, in four steps.

What happens between your appointments, on the screens your clients actually use.

1

Your client photographs the plate.

No recall, no food diary that dies in week two, no typing. Packaged food is scanned from the Nutrition Facts panel and transcribed rather than estimated — estimation is reserved for plates that need it.

2

The model reads it, and asks when it can't see.

Each food carries high, medium or low confidence. When something invisible would move the numbers — cooking fat, a sauce, food off-frame — it asks one to three questions rather than guessing. Estimates are bounded by an energy-consistency check so one bad read can't distort a day.

3

It lands as adherence you can compare.

Timing is part of the record: on time counts full, late counts half, and the log says how late. You set the targets. You can never move the weights, and each has a published ceiling — which is what makes adherence comparable across your whole book.

4

You choose how much structure they see.

You are one of only three roles that can assign a plan style. Put a client on Intuitive and the app shows them no calories and no macros at all — scoring awareness, adequate fueling, hydration and consistency instead. Awareness is scored on answering, not on the answer: a 1-out-of-5 satisfaction scores exactly like a 5.

What it will not do: it does not diagnose, deliver medical nutrition therapy, or set a target. It can flag a possible allergen conflict from what it detects and what the client declared — it can never confirm a plate is allergen-free, and it says so on the screen.

RD questions, straight answers

Asked by professionals who've been burned by "AI nutrition" before.

How accurate is the AI, really?

It's honest about being an estimator. Detections carry per‑food confidence, portions are estimates and labeled as such, and outputs are bounded by sanity clamps so a misread can't blow up a day. When something invisible would move the macros, it asks the client instead of guessing — and packaged foods skip estimation entirely via exact label transcription. Treat it as a very good field record, not a metabolic ward.

Can it tell a client a meal is safe for their allergy?

No, and it will never claim to. Declared restrictions are checked on every scan and possible conflicts get flagged as a heads‑up — but a photo can't reveal sauces, oils, cross‑contact, or hidden ingredients, and the platform says exactly that. Clients with serious allergies are told to treat it as one more set of eyes, not a guarantee.

Does it replace my clinical judgment?

It's built not to. The AI gives execution feedback, not medical advice — its own terms say so. It never sets targets, never touches the score, and never frames food morally. What it gives you is the thing you've never had: a trustworthy record of what clients actually ate, so your judgment works on real data.

How do clients join me?

You set up a practice and get a client code. Clients download the app, sign up free, enter your code once, and appear on your book as requests you approve. Their goal at signup sets their scoring profile, and their declared allergies follow them onto every scan.
