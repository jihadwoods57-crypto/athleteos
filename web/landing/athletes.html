What is OnStandard

The proof behind your work.

OnStandard is where your daily execution becomes a number people can trust. Your coach or trainer sets your standard — meals, recovery, check‑ins. You log by pointing the camera at the plate; an AI reads the food honestly and your score moves the moment the work lands. The people invested in you see the result, live.

  • Not a tracker.Trackers record what you type in. OnStandard verifies what you actually did, from a photo — and repeats score nothing.
  • Not a social app.No feed, no followers. Teammates see one number on a leaderboard; the details stay between you, your coach, and the standard.
  • Yours forever.Leave a team and your history — every logged day, score, and thread — goes with you, free and readable.

The daily loop

A day on the standard.

Around ninety seconds of logging, spread across a day you were already living. The score does the talking after that.

7:40 AM

Point the camera at breakfast

That's the log. The AI identifies the foods, estimates portions and macros, and tags each detection with a confidence level. If it can't see something that matters — sauce, a hidden side — it asks one or two quick questions instead of guessing.

"The camera can't see everything. Answer these and your read is dead on."
7:41 AM

The score moves

Your day lands against your standard. On‑time meals count full, late counts half, skipped reads as skipped. The home screen shows the ring, your streak, and what's still open — plus what today can still reach if you finish it.

"Good job getting breakfast in on time. That's the standard."
1:15 PM

Your coach weighs in — with context

Every logged meal opens a thread: the AI's read first, then your coach's comment, then you. You can ask the AI coach questions and it answers from your plan and your day — not the internet. When your coach opens your day, you see "Seen by Coach." The watching goes both ways.

9:30 PM

Twenty seconds locks your recovery

The nightly check‑in — energy, recovery, sleep, confidence — is a quarter of your score, and it only counts if you actually do it. Self‑reported, so the app says it straight: what you enter becomes your score, keep it honest.

12:00 AM

The day locks

80 or better and your streak grows. The rules are real: one grace day per rolling week, absent days count as misses, and the calendar is the judge. That's exactly why a long streak means something.

"80 is the bar. On standard means 80+. Not close, not almost."

Athlete questions, straight answers

What athletes ask before they put their days on the record.

Is it actually free for me?

Yes. You never pay while you're attached to a coach, trainer, or program — they carry the cost because they get the value. Full app, full AI, full history. If you leave, your record doesn't vanish; it's yours.

How do I join?

Your coach or trainer gives you a code. You sign up as an athlete, enter the code once, and your work starts counting toward their board the moment they approve you. Training solo with a trainer instead of a team? Same flow — trainer codes work identically.

I'm under 18. What happens?

Under 13 can't sign up at all — that's the law and we hold it. Under 18, your data stays on your phone until a parent or guardian approves by email: one email, one approve button. They never see your meals or photos; approving just lets your score reach your coach.

What if I have a bad day?

It reads as a bad day — and one is survivable. Your streak carries one grace day per rolling week, late meals still earn half, and the breakdown shows exactly what to fix tomorrow. What the app won't do is pretend it didn't happen.

Can I make my macros look better than they are?

You can edit the AI's read before logging — it's your plate and sometimes you know better. But the photo is attached, your coach sees it, duplicates score zero, and the score is clamped to what's actually logged. The system is built so honesty is the easy path.

What happens when I leave my team?

Your history stays yours — every logged day, score, and thread, free and readable. Live scoring pauses until you connect with your next coach, trainer, or program, and they see your track record from day one.
