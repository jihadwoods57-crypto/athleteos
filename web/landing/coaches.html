What is OnStandard

The standard, with receipts.

OnStandard is an execution and accountability platform. You set the standard for your team — meals, recovery check‑ins, weigh‑in days, requirements — and every athlete proves they hit it with photo‑verified logs an AI reads honestly. One score per athlete per day, computed by the same fixed math for everyone, live on your dashboard the moment work lands.

  • You own the standard.Targets, meal windows, weigh‑in days, and requirements — tuned per room and per athlete, within evidence‑based rails.
  • The platform owns the math.Fixed weights, published tiers, an honesty clamp. No athlete can sweet‑talk the score, and no coach can hollow it out.
  • The proof is a photo.Meals are logged by pointing a camera at the plate. Repeated photos are caught by hash and score nothing.

Verified Commitments

For the things a photo can't prove.

Schedule a 5 AM roll call, a lift, study hall, tutoring or rehab. The athlete gets a card carrying your own title for it — "5 AM Club", not software vocabulary — and one button. Reminders go only to athletes who haven't answered, so nobody is called out in a group chat, and they deliberately break quiet hours because a silent 4:45 AM alarm is a feature that doesn't work.

It produces its own Accountability number, separate from the daily score: answering counts a little, arriving on time counts more, finishing counts most. "Couldn't verify" is never "missed" — a dead phone or weak GPS leaves the calculation rather than counting against anyone, and only you can mark someone missed.

Arrival verification watches a single location you specify, only during that event's window. No coordinates are ever stored, and athletes who decline background location check in with a button instead.

Trust Passes — earned, not given

Seven photo-proven days at 80+ earns an athlete camera-free days, credited at the median of their own proven days — never their best one. Every fifth day the camera returns as a spot-check, and after ten camera-free days the credit decays. You grant it; you can end it.

Coach questions, straight answers

The things coaches ask before they put their room on it.

How do my athletes get on it?

You finish setup and get a team code. Send it to the group chat; each athlete enters it once during their own signup and lands on your board as a join request you approve. Athletes under 18 additionally need one‑email parent approval before anything syncs to you.

What exactly do I see for each athlete?

Their daily score and flag, every logged meal with its photo and the AI's macro read, recovery check‑ins, weight trend, requirement completion, and the meal threads. Opening an athlete's day is read‑only. You get two messages per meal — a deliberate cap, so the ones you send carry weight. Reactions are unlimited.

Can athletes game the photo proof?

Every photo is hashed; a re‑used photo stays visible to you but scores zero. Late logs count half, empty days read as empty, and the score is clamped so it can never exceed what was actually logged. If they want the number, they have to do the work.

Do I have to set macros for every kid?

No. Every athlete starts on your team default the moment they join. When you want to individualize, the targets editor suggests protein and calories from open math — bulk, cut, or hold — that you approve before it saves. Adjust one athlete or leave the default; the room keeps running either way.

I have assistants and a dietitian. Can they see the roster?

Yes. You invite assistants and a nutritionist/RD with single‑use staff codes; they get the same live board. Revoke a staff seat anytime.

What does it cost, and who pays?

You pay; your athletes never do. Programs and facilities run $249/mo up to 30 active participants, $499 up to 75, $799 up to 150, with departments and multi-location quoted. An independent coach on their own book is $99/mo up to 25 clients or $179/mo up to 50. Every plan carries a 14-day trial and cancels in account settings — see pricing.
