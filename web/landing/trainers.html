What is OnStandard

Your standard, running while you sleep.

OnStandard is an execution platform for the people you train. You set up your practice, share one client code, and every client's daily execution — meals logged by camera, recovery checked nightly — lands on your book as an honest score. Built for athletes and everyday clients: the scoring adapts to their goal, whether that's performance, fat loss, or muscle gain.

  • The default client standard.Three meals with photo proof, a nightly recovery check‑in, weight as a weekly trend, a weekly check‑in. Set once at signup, applied to every client.
  • Goal‑aware scoring.A fat‑loss client is scored on calorie‑window adherence; a muscle‑gain client on hitting the calorie floor; an athlete on protein and timing. Same honest math, tuned to the goal.
  • Never a fabricated number.A client who didn't log shows "No logs today" — not an invented score. What you see is what happened.

Trainer questions, straight answers

What trainers ask before moving their book over.

How do my clients get on?

You get a client code when you set up your practice. Clients sign up free, enter your code once, and appear on your book as requests you approve. They can also find your practice by name in the app's directory and request from there.

Do my clients pay anything?

No. Clients are free while they're attached to your practice — you carry the subscription because you get the book‑wide view. If a client leaves, their history stays theirs.

What exactly do I see for each client?

Their daily score, recovery and readiness from the nightly check‑in, and nutrition consistency — live. The scope is shown to clients in the app in plain words, which is why they log honestly. Deeper coaching surfaces (meal photo review, targets editing, requirement assignment) live in the coach lane today.

I train regular people, not athletes. Does the scoring fit?

Yes — this is exactly what the goal profiles are for. Fat‑loss and maintenance clients are scored primarily on calorie‑window adherence and meal consistency; muscle‑gain clients on hitting their calorie floor and protein. Same framework, same honesty, tuned to the actual goal.

What happens when a client stops logging?

You see it immediately — "No logs today" on your book, never a fabricated score. That's the moment your note‑as‑push earns its keep: one sentence from you, on their phone, before the quiet week becomes a quiet month.

I'm a dietitian, not a trainer. Which page is mine?

You have your own: OnStandard for dietitians & nutritionists. Short version — solo RDs run a practice exactly like this, and team dietitians join a coach's staff with a "Nutritionist / RD" seat.
