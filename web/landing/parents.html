What is OnStandard

The standard their coach set. The proof they hit it.

OnStandard is the app your athlete's coach or trainer runs their standard on. Your athlete logs meals by photo, checks in on recovery, and earns one honest daily score computed by the same fixed math as every athlete on the platform. It exists so the people invested in a young athlete — coach, family — can trust the work without hovering over it.

  • Not a monitoring app.You get the outcome — score, streaks, completion — never a window into their plates, their weight, or their private check‑ins.
  • Not a social network.No feed, no followers, no strangers. Only the people your athlete connects can see anything at all.
  • Never sold.No ad tracking, no third‑party analytics identifiers. The score is the product — not your child's data.

The consent gate

Under 18, you hold the key.

This isn't a settings toggle buried three menus deep. For any athlete under 18, OnStandard is built so their data physically stays on their phone until a parent or guardian approves. Denied by default — the platform refuses so you don't have to.

Reading the number

A 90 you can trust more than "it went fine."

The score isn't a mood or a self‑report. It's computed from photo‑verified meals, real check‑ins, and fixed math that's the same for every athlete in the country — and it's clamped so it can never exceed what was actually logged.

Proven, not promised

Meals are logged by pointing a camera at the plate, and an AI reads what's actually there. Re‑used photos are caught and score nothing. When the number says the work happened, it happened.

Streaks that mean something

A streak is consecutive days at 80 or better, with one grace day per rolling week and no loophole for going quiet. A 26‑day streak is 26 real days — the kind of consistency you've been hoping to see, made visible.

A record they'll keep

Every proven day builds a history that belongs to your athlete forever — through team changes, school changes, the next level. Discipline, documented, is an asset no one can take back.

Parent questions, straight answers

The things parents ask before a family gets on. No hedging.

How do I get access?

Parents join from their athlete's invite — there's no separate parent signup, by design. If your athlete is under 18, you'll get the approval email first; visibility follows from that link. If their coach isn't on OnStandard yet, send the coach to onstandard.app/coaches — that's the fastest path for the whole team, and athletes ride free.

Can I see what my child eats, or their weight?

No — and that's a promise, not a limitation. Parents see score, streaks, and completion only. Meal photos never leave the coach connection, weight is visible only as a season trend to the coach, and check‑in answers stay private. The app tells your athlete this in plain words, which is why they trust it enough to use it.

My child is under 13. Can they use it?

No. Signup requires being 13 or older, and an under‑13 birthdate hits a hard block — the app doesn't store their identity and tells them to come back on their 13th birthday. We'd rather lose a signup than cut a corner on children's privacy law.

Is my child's data sold or used for ads?

No. No ad tracking, no third‑party analytics identifiers, no data sales. Meal photos live in private storage scoped to the coach connection. Your athlete can export or delete everything from their account — and parents of minors can request full data access or deletion at any time.

What does it cost our family?

Nothing, in the normal case: athletes are free while they're attached to a paying coach, trainer, or program. The professional pays because the professional gets the roster value.

What if I approve and later change my mind?

Consent is yours to withdraw. Guardian approval can be revoked, and parents of minors can request full data access or deletion at any time via support@onstandard.app. Your athlete keeps their own record either way — it's theirs.
