1. Who this policy is for

OnStandard is an athlete nutrition-accountability platform for athletes and the people who support them (parents/guardians, personal trainers, sports-performance coaches, nutritionists, and high-school and college coaches). The app's primary users are athletes roughly ages 13–22. Some athletes are minors. We treat athlete nutrition, weight, and check-in data as sensitive and apply heightened protections to minors' data (see §7 and §8).

2. Two modes: on-device vs. connected

  • On-device only. When the app's backend is not enabled, the app runs entirely on your device using local storage. In this state, no personal data is sent to or collected by our servers.
  • Connected (backend enabled). When the backend is enabled and you sign in, your data is stored on our hosted infrastructure so it can sync across your devices and be shared with the people you have linked, subject to the consent rules in §7.

3. What we collect (connected state)

  • Account and identity: name, email, role (athlete / parent / trainer / coach / nutritionist), and — for athletes — sport, position, school or program/organization name, primary goal, training frequency, and the age you enter at setup.
  • Nutrition and health data: meals you log and their estimated or transcribed macros; meal photos and Nutrition Facts label photos you capture (see §4); daily accountability scores; body weight, starting weight, and weight goals; hydration; weekly check-in answers; and performance records (PRs) you log.
  • Relationships and links: the coaches, trainers, nutritionists, and guardians you link to; team/join codes; and, for staff accounts, the athletes on your roster or client book.
  • Messages you send to linked people through the app.
  • Authentication data: your sign-in is handled by our authentication provider; we store your account identifier and email. We never store your password in the app.
  • We do NOT collect advertising identifiers, cross-app tracking data, location data, or analytics from third-party advertising/tracking SDKs. The app contains no third-party analytics, advertising, attribution, or crash-tracking SDKs.

4. Meal-photo and label analysis by a third-party AI (Anthropic / Claude)

When AI meal analysis is configured, a photo you take of a meal or a Nutrition Facts label is sent to our AI provider, Anthropic (the Claude API), to identify foods and estimate or transcribe nutrition information. This is the only path by which a photo or meal data leaves your device to a third party.

  • The photo is sent only when (a) AI analysis is configured and (b) a consent gate passes. The gate fails closed: an athlete without consent, an athlete who has paused sharing, and a minor whose guardian is not verified never have a photo sent off the device.
  • The Anthropic API key is held only on our server; it is never in the app.
  • We use the AI to provide the nutrition feature. We do not use your photos for advertising.
  • Two in-app text features may send already-computed text and numbers (no photos) to Anthropic purely to reword them in a coach voice; the app re-verifies the numbers and never lets the AI change them.

Under Anthropic’s commercial API terms, data we submit (including meal photos) is not used to train Anthropic’s models, and API inputs and outputs are retained only for a limited period for trust-and-safety monitoring before deletion.

5. Not medical advice

OnStandard provides nutrition education and accountability, not medical advice. AI meal estimates, scores, and coaching copy are estimates and may be inaccurate. The app is not a substitute for a physician or registered dietitian. If you or someone you support is struggling with eating or body image, help is available: in the U.S., the National Alliance for Eating Disorders helpline at 1-866-662-1235, or call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline).

6. How we use your data

We use your data to provide the service: analyze and log meals, compute and show your accountability score and trends, let the people you have linked see what you have agreed to share, generate roster/compliance views for your coach or trainer, and send the reminders you enable. We do not sell your personal information, and we do not share it for cross-context behavioral advertising.

7. Who can see your data, and your sharing controls

  • You can always see and export your own data.
  • People you link can see the athlete data you have consented to share — e.g. your daily score, compliance, and weight trend.
  • Consent is required and revocable. Real athlete data is only collected and shared after consent is recorded. You can pause all sharing at any time from the Profile screen, which immediately stops data leaving the device, and you can remove a linked viewer's access.
  • Service providers (subprocessors): our hosting/database/storage and authentication provider (Supabase), our email provider (Resend) for account emails, and Anthropic for the AI analysis in §4. Each operates under contract.
  • Legal disclosure: we may disclose data if required by law or to protect users (especially minors).

8. Minors and parental/guardian consent

  • Under 13 are barred. The minimum signup age is 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13; if we learn we have, we will delete it.
  • Ages 13–17 require verified guardian consent before any data is shared. For an athlete under 18, the app keeps the athlete's real data on the device only until a parent or guardian is verifiably approved. A self-tapped checkbox by the minor is not sufficient; the gate fails closed.
  • Minor safety in messaging. A registered minor cannot be placed in an unsupervised message thread with an unrelated adult; minor messaging is limited to the minor's authorized coach, trainer, or guardian.
  • Guardian rights. A parent or guardian can review their minor's data, request its deletion, and withdraw consent at any time using the in-app controls or by contacting support@onstandard.app.

9. Education records (FERPA)

Where a school, team, or coach provides rosters, or where OnStandard handles data that may constitute education records, we handle that data to provide the service and do not sell it or use it for unrelated purposes.

10. Your rights: access, export, and deletion

  • Export. You can export a structured (JSON) copy of your own data from the Account screen.
  • Deletion. You can permanently delete your account and data from the app. Deletion removes your server account and cascades to delete the data you own (days, meals, check-ins, links, threads) and your meal-photo storage, and wipes your local on-device data.
  • GDPR / CCPA-CPRA. EU/UK and California residents have additional rights (access, correction, deletion, portability, and opt-out of sale/sharing). We do not sell or share personal information as those terms are used by CCPA/CPRA. To exercise any right, use the in-app controls or email support@onstandard.app.

11. Data retention

We keep your data while your account is active. When you delete your account, we delete your personal data and meal photos as described in §10, and complete deletion within 30 days.

12. Security

  • The app stores your authenticated session token in the device's encrypted OS keychain, not in plain storage.
  • Data is transmitted over encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS), and the database enforces row-level security so that only you and the people you have linked can read your data.
  • The app bundle contains no secret keys. The public API key shipped in the app is a publishable key gated by server-side row-level security; the Anthropic key lives only on the server.
  • Meal photos are sent over an encrypted connection to our server and to Anthropic for analysis, where they are processed for that purpose. No system is perfectly secure.

13. International transfers

OnStandard operates from the United States, and our service providers (including Supabase and Anthropic) process data in the U.S. If you use the Service from outside the U.S., you understand that your information is transferred to and processed in the U.S. Where applicable law requires a transfer mechanism, we rely on our providers’ standard contractual protections.

14. Changes to this policy

We will post changes here and update the effective date. We will provide notice of material changes; material changes affecting minors will be communicated to guardians.
