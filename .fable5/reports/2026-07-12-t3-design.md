## The problem, grounded

Screenshotted the live strip at 390×844 (score 0) and rendered the app's own `scoreRing()` at 0 / 68 / 94 to see every value. Findings:

1. **The "star glyph" is an orphan, not a design.** The ring's comet-tip sparkle is drawn at the *leading edge of the arc*. At mid/high scores it reads beautifully (it rides the gradient arc). At **score 0 the arc is empty, so the spark floats alone at 12 o'clock** on a blank ring and reads as decoration, not data. That is the "off" the ticket named.
2. **The tier pill is literally unstyled at the starting tier.** `tier(0)` returns `cls:'r'`, but `.status-pill.r` **has no CSS** (`app.css` only defines `.g/.a/.b/.p`). So "Off Standard" renders as plain white text with no pill — no status read at all.
3. **Redundant number + cryptic meta.** The big "0" sits *beside* the ring (the ring already implies a score), and the right meta "`0 of 4 in` / `0 → 63`" is cramped 10.5px text where "`0 → 63`" is unexplained.
4. **Flat next to its neighbor.** The strip looks unintentional directly above the confident, colorful NOW card.

## The redesign

A single consolidated **score dial**. Every decision is derived from the existing token system (`css/tokens.css`) — same ring gradient stops (`#A3E635 → #34D399 → #22D3EE → #3B82F6`), same surfaces, same `status-pill` / `stk-pill` families, same radii and motion curves. No per-screen style fork.

- **Number moves inside the ring.** One focal point instead of ring + redundant sibling number. The dial is the *same component language* as the celebration hero, just compact — the prototype's "100" state shows the strip dial and the hero dial are one system at two sizes.
- **Empty-ring-at-0 fixed.** At score < 6 the track becomes a dim dotted "ready" ring and the leading spark is **not drawn**; it only appears once there is arc to lead. The spark is never orphaned, and a full ring (100) lets it complete the loop as the reward.
- **Honest tier pill at every tier**, including a real muted-red treatment for "Off Standard" (`#FF9B9B` on `--red-surface`/`--red-border`). Building amber, Locked In blue, OnStandard green — reusing the existing pill tokens.
- **Requirements labeled, not just dotted.** Thicker rounded segment bars + "`N of 4 in`".
- **Projection clarified.** The cryptic "`0 → 63`" becomes "`… · reach 63 today`" — same number, honest headroom framing, folded onto the requirement line so it clears the right edge and never collides with the streak pill.

### Data integrity
Presentation only. `score`, `met`, `total`, and the projected `possible` all pass straight through from `S.exec`; the tier name is unchanged; the streak pill still reflects `S.streak`. Nothing about scoring logic is touched.

### SETTLED decisions honored
- `strip()` stays the single tap target owning `data-go="score-breakdown"`; the redesign keeps one strip, no nested `data-go`. Any future surface remains a **sibling** (per the 2026-07-11 pattern).
- Streak pill keeps its grace-calibrated tiers (COVERED / SECURED); the redesign only restyles, never re-litigates the urgency copy.

## Two variants (toggle in the prototype)

- **Variant A — Dial-first (RECOMMENDED).** 64px ring is the anchor, number inside; tier + streak on top, segment bars + labeled projection below. Best because the box's tap target *is* the score breakdown and the tab *is* the score home — leading with the dial keeps one mental model from compact strip to full hero.
- **Variant B — Progress-first.** Smaller 48px companion ring; a prominent fat progress meter is the centerpiece, teaching "what's left today" before the number. Legitimate alternative fitting "athletes are taught, not just scored" — pick it only if the daily strip should coach tasks over headlining the score.
