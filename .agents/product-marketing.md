## Product Overview
**One-liner:** OnStandard turns daily execution into credible proof, visible to the people counting on you.

**What it does:** An execution and accountability platform for athletes, everyday clients, and the coaches, trainers, and dietitians who guide them. The professional sets the standard; the athlete proves they hit it — meals, recovery, and check-ins — with photo-verified logs an AI reads honestly. One shared score shows what actually got done, live, to everyone invested in that athlete.

**Product category:** Athlete execution & accountability platform. Explicitly NOT a fitness tracker, NOT a social app, NOT an NIL marketplace. (This "what we are not" framing is core positioning.)

**Product type:** B2B2C SaaS — mobile (iOS, Android) + web. Professionals are the buyers; athletes/clients are the users.

**Business model:** The people who profit pay; the people who sweat don't.
- **Athletes & clients:** $0 while on a roster (full app, full AI, full history, portable forever).
- **Professional (trainers, RDs, private coaches):** $124.99/mo, 50 client seats included. Founding 50 lock 50% off for 12 months, grandfathered.
- **Programs & facilities:** from $249/mo, usage-based (pay only for active athletes; idle seats free up). 30 to 150+ athletes. 30-day trial at onboarding.

## Target Audience
**Buyers (who pays):** Coaches, personal trainers, registered dietitians (RDs), and programs/schools/performance gyms.

**Users (who logs):** Athletes (from Power-4 college down through youth club), everyday training clients, and parents (visibility, not logging).

**Primary use case:** Prove what actually happens in "the other 167 hours" — the time between sessions and practices that determines results but that no one can currently see.

**Jobs to be done:**
- See who on the roster is executing — and who's slipping — without chasing anyone.
- Protect the money/time/opportunity invested in an athlete's development.
- (Athlete) Walk into every room — recruiting, scholarship, contract — with receipts.

**Use cases / scenarios:**
- Coach checks the whole roster in ~3 seconds each morning: who's on standard, who needs attention, every log as it lands.
- Trainer/RD keeps clients compliant between weekly sessions instead of losing them to drift.
- Program aligns multiple staff and rolls scores up across coaches and teams.

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Coaches plan brilliantly, athletes promise sincerely, and nobody can prove what actually happened between sessions.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Trackers only record what you *type in* — self-reported, unverified.
- Text chains, spreadsheets, and manual check-ins don't scale to full rosters and get missed.
- Social/fitness apps add feeds and noise instead of proof.

**What it costs them:** Missed habits, eroded trust, wasted scholarship/NIL/development investment, and athletes who don't get the opportunity because consistency couldn't be shown.

**Emotional tension:** The stakes just rose — NIL, revenue sharing, and the portal raised the financial stakes of athlete development while shrinking the margin for error. Talent is common; consistency, discipline, and availability separate the ones who get paid. Coaches feel they can't track it all; athletes feel their work is invisible.

## Competitive Landscape
The honest read: **no one else verifies daily execution from a photo and scores it on one fixed, shared formula.** Everyone in the space relies on self-report. That's the wedge. The competition is best mapped by *which persona* they compete for.

**Direct — closest on the job (prove daily habits to the person guiding you), but all self-reported:**
- **RD / nutrition-coaching apps** — Healthie, Practice Better, RXFood, Rise. These give dietitians a client food journal + messaging (RXFood even does photo meal logging with RD review). They own the trainer/RD persona today. Gap: no verification standard, no fixed cross-client score, built for the practitioner's back office, not the athlete's daily proof.
- **Athlete Management Systems (AMS)** — Smartabase, CoachMePlus, TeamBuildr's readiness/wellness. Colleges and pro teams use these for daily wellness/readiness check-ins. Gap: self-reported questionnaires, no photo proof, no nutrition truth, priced and built for high-end performance staff.

**Secondary — same problem, weaker approach:**
- Self-report trackers: MyFitnessPal, MacroFactor, Cronometer — record what the user types; no coach loop, no verification, no shared standard.
- Manual accountability: spreadsheets, GroupMe/text chains, weekly check-ins — don't verify and don't scale to a roster.
- Strength/programming platforms: TrainHeroic, Volt, Bridge Athletic — own the *workout* but not the between-session execution.

**Indirect — adjacent but a different job:**
- Doing nothing / trust-based coaching (the real default for most coaches).
- NIL / athlete-ops platforms: Opendorse, INFLCR, Teamworks. They monetize or operate the athlete; OnStandard is the daily *proof* those opportunities quietly depend on, not the marketplace.

**The one-line frame for "vs" pages:** *They record what you say. OnStandard proves what you did.*

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- **Photo-verified, not self-reported.** Live capture only; gallery photos never score. AI reads foods, portions, macros with per-food confidence and shows a "?" instead of guessing.
- **One fixed, published formula.** A 94 means the same thing on every roster in the country. Weights are fixed (Nutrition 50%, Recovery 25%, Daily commitment 15%, Weekly check-in 10%); the professional individualizes the *standard underneath* within evidence-based rails. No coach can invent an easy formula; no athlete can sweet-talk the math.
- **AI reads but never decides.** It never sets targets, never touches the score, never invents a number a person acts on. Fixed math scores the day; the professional sets the bar.
- **Honest credit.** On time counts full, late counts half, skipped reads as skipped.
- **Portable, athlete-owned record** — yours forever, even after you leave. Programs get access, never ownership.

**Why customers choose us:** It's the only place the daily work is *proven* rather than claimed, in a currency (the score) that's trusted because nobody can game it.

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Is this surveillance of the athlete?" | The athlete owns the record forever; parents support with confidence, not surveillance. The score talks to the people already invested in you — no public feed, no shame. |
| "Won't the AI get my food wrong?" | Per-food confidence on every plate; a "?" instead of a guess. It never confirms a plate is allergen-free. A human (coach + athlete) is always in the thread. |
| "Another app/dashboard to manage" | The whole roster reads in ~3 seconds with needs-attention flags; it surfaces only what matters. |
| "Why pay when trackers are free?" | Trackers record claims. OnStandard produces proof — and for athletes it's $0. |

**Anti-persona:** Anyone wanting a social fitness feed, gamified badges, leaderboard competition, or a casual self-tracking app. OnStandard rewards executing the plan, not opening the app.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Can't see what happens between sessions; rosters too large to track by hand; critical habits slip through.
**Pull:** Verified proof, one trustworthy score, the whole roster at a glance.
**Habit:** Text chains, spreadsheets, and trusting that the work got done.
**Anxiety:** Is the AI accurate? Is this surveillance? Is it one more tool that won't get used?
